Latest News on Autonomous Vehicles

  • Rivian to Launch "Eyes-Off" System by 2026: Rivian has revealed ambitious plans to introduce a next-generation "eyes-off" autonomous driving system by 2026. The company also aims to release a hands-free advanced driver assistance system in 2025. These technologies are designed to provide drivers with the ability to relax or focus on non-driving activities during long trips. Read Source
  • China Leads in the Global Robotaxi Market: Chinese companies are dominating the race to bring robotaxis to the market, leveraging the country's advanced electric vehicle (EV) manufacturing capabilities. By integrating autonomous driving technology into EVs at a lower cost, companies like WeRide are gaining a competitive edge. Experts predict a significant expansion of robotaxi fleets in China over the next five years. Read Source
  • DHL Implements Autonomous Trucks in Texas: DHL Supply Chain is pioneering logistics innovation by deploying autonomous Volvo trucks in Texas. These trucks are operating on two dedicated routes, enhancing efficiency and demonstrating the potential for autonomous technology in freight transportation. DHL aims to expand this program to other regions based on the success of the Texas pilot. Read Source
  • Beijing Unveils Plans for Autonomous Public Transit: Starting April 2025, Beijing will roll out new regulations promoting the use of driverless buses and taxis. After rigorous safety evaluations, these autonomous vehicles will become an integral part of the city's public transportation network, setting a benchmark for other global cities aiming to adopt similar systems. Read Source
  • UK's First Driverless Bus Service Ends in 2025: Despite being the world's first driverless bus service, the route between Fife and Edinburgh will be discontinued in February 2025 due to low passenger numbers. Launched in May 2023, the project provided invaluable insights into the feasibility and challenges of autonomous public transport, laying the groundwork for future developments. Read Source
