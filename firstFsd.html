Ralph Teetor


Ralph Teetor (August 17, 1890 – February 15, 1982) was a prolific inventor who invented cruise control. He was the longtime president of the automotive parts manufacturer The Perfect Circle Co. (acquired in 1963 by Dana Holding Corporation, then sold to Mahle GmbH in 2007) in Hagerstown, Indiana, a manufacturer of piston rings.


Born: August 17, 1890, Hagerstown, Indiana, USA

Died: February 15, 1982, Richmond, Indiana, USA

Known for: Inventing cruise control

Early Life

Ralph Teetor was born in Hagerstown, Indiana. He became blind at the age of 5 due to an injury but went on to excel in engineering, earning a degree from the University of Pennsylvania in 1912.

Career

Teetor worked with the Teetor-Hartley Motor Company and later developed a technique for balancing steam turbine rotors during World War I. He was president of the Perfect Circle Company, which manufactured piston rings.

Cruise Control

Teetor invented cruise control, initially called "Speedostat", after being annoyed by a lawyer's erratic driving. His patent for this device was filed in 1950, and it became popular in cars as "Cruise Control" in the 1950s.

Other Achievements

In addition to cruise control, Teetor invented a powered lawn mower, lock mechanisms, and other devices. He overcame his blindness to become a successful engineer and entrepreneur.
