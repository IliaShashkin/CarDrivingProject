Level 1

Driver Assistance

Adaptive Cruise Control (ACC).
The system controls either
steering or\acceleration/braking
(but not both simultaneously).
The driver must always keep their
hands on the wheel
and monitor the road.

Level 2

Partial Automation

Example: Tesla Autopilot (2.0),
Lane Keeping Assist (LKA) systems.
The vehicle can simultaneously manage
steering and acceleration/braking.
The driver must remain ready
to take control at any moment.

Level 3

Conditional Automation

Example: Audi A8 with Traffic Jam Pilot,
    Tesla Autopilot (3.0),
The system can take full control in specific
conditions (e.g., in traffic jams).
The driver can be distracted but must be
ready to intervene if prompted by the system.

FSD

High Automation

    Example: Robotaxi prototypes (Waymo, Yandex,
advanced Tesla FSD Beta versions).
The vehicle can fully drive itself in specific
geofenced areas and conditions without driver intervention.
The driver can completely relax,
but the system is limited to predefined scenarios.

What is that

Autonomous driving is a feature of offered by certain automotive companies like Tesla, Audi, BMW etc.
This feature allows the driver to use autonomous driving/assistance to help drive their vehicles. First instance of autonomous assistance was in 1948, where Ralph Teetor invented the first cruise-control system.
However, major progress began in the 1980's where, via the funding from both the US government and German government, the first semi-autonomous driving system was developed.
Through later developments in the early 2000's and 2015, the first autonomous driving system was developed.
From 2017 onwards, companies like Waymo developed privately researched autonomous driving systems. And then, further advancements made by Tesla in the 2020's, Full Self Driving (FDS) systems were now capable of reliably driving without human input.
