In 2000, the development of autonomous vehicles was still in its early stages. 2015
Breakthroughs and Key Advancements in Autonomous Vehiclesr

In 2015, significant progress was made in the field of autonomous vehicles. Tesla introduced its Autopilot feature, a major step toward self-driving cars for consumers, offering semi-autonomous capabilities such as lane-keeping and adaptive cruise control. Google’s self-driving car project, which later became Waymo, also reached new milestones with successful road tests and the development of fully autonomous vehicles. The industry saw increased investment, collaboration, and testing, setting the stage for future innovations in autonomous driving technology.

2017
Expanding Capabilities and Industry Milestones

In 2017, autonomous vehicle technology continued to evolve, with several key developments. Waymo, the self-driving car project from Google, began offering rides to the public in its fully autonomous minivans without steering wheels or pedals in Phoenix, Arizona. Tesla's Autopilot saw significant updates, improving its semi-autonomous driving capabilities, although still requiring driver supervision. Major automotive companies like General Motors, Ford, and Volvo increased their investments in autonomous vehicle technology, while regulatory discussions and safety concerns began to take center stage. This year marked a turning point in the transition from research to real-world testing and early adoption.

2020
Acceleration in Testing and Industry Integration

In 2020, autonomous vehicle technology made substantial strides toward broader adoption. Waymo expanded its fully autonomous taxi service to a larger area in Phoenix, Arizona, continuing to refine its self-driving systems. Tesla's Autopilot and Full Self-Driving (FSD) features became more advanced, offering improved navigation on highways and city streets, though still requiring driver oversight. Companies like Cruise (owned by GM) and Aurora began testing autonomous trucks, signaling growth beyond passenger vehicles. Amid the COVID-19 pandemic, there was increased interest in contactless delivery services, with several companies, including Nuro and Postmates, launching autonomous delivery vehicles for food and goods. The focus shifted from development to real-world applications and safety validation.
