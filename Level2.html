Tesla Autopilot 2.0


Tesla Autopilot 2.0 is a semi-autonomous system introduced in 2016 that manages steering, acceleration, and braking. It uses a suite of sensors to enable adaptive cruise control, lane centering, and automated lane changes. The system still requires the driver to stay alert and take control when needed.



Lane Keeping Assist (LKA)

Lane Keeping Assist (LKA) helps drivers stay within their lanes by gently correcting the vehicle's steering. Using cameras and sensors to detect lane markings, this system is especially useful in highway driving but requires driver oversight to ensure safety.
